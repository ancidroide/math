# Integer Exponents

Summary of integer exponents rules and properties from Paul's Online Math Notes.

## Definition
For any real number $a$ and positive integer $n$:
$a^n = a \cdot a \cdot \dots \cdot a$ ($n$ times)

Parentheses are crucial when dealing with negative bases:
- $(-2)^4 = (-2)(-2)(-2)(-2) = 16$
- $-2^4 = -(2^4) = -16$

## Zero and Negative Exponents
- $a^0 = 1$ (for $a \neq 0$)
- $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$ (for $a \neq 0$)

## Properties
- $a^n \cdot a^m = a^{n+m}$
- $(a^n)^m = a^{nm}$
- $\frac{a^n}{a^m} = \begin{cases} a^{n-m} & \text{if } n > m \\ \frac{1}{a^{m-n}} & \text{if } m > n \end{cases}$
- $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$
- $(\frac{a}{b})^n = \frac{a^n}{b^n}$ (for $b \neq 0$)
- $(\frac{a}{b})^{-n} = (\frac{b}{a})^n = \frac{b^n}{a^n}$
- $\frac{1}{a^{-n}} = a^n$
- $\frac{a^{-n}}{b^{-m}} = \frac{b^m}{a^n}$
- $(\frac{a^n}{b^m})^k = \frac{a^{nk}}{b^{mk}}$
